---
name: align-coupled
description: Find places where one reality is carried in more than one place and make those parts stronger together. Use when artifacts have drifted apart, when scattered agreement is no longer maintained, or when the project depends on correspondences that nobody is actively keeping true.
argument-hint: "[focus area or shaping direction]"
---

Work on the parts of this project where one reality is being carried in more than one place, and make those parts stronger together.

Use any focus, constraint, or shaping direction you are given as the place to enter. If none is given, find the places where the project is depending on scattered agreement that is no longer being actively maintained, and improve those places.

Look for cases where behavior, meaning, or trust depends on several artifacts continuing to correspond: code and the examples that explain it, prompts and the places that invoke them, workflows and the logic around them, scenarios and the behavior they are meant to protect, sidecar writing and the structures it claims exist, names and the concepts they are supposed to hold steady, boundaries and the powers actually exercised across them, projections and the source surfaces they are supposed to reveal. Look for the places where several things are, in practice, one thing, but have started to drift apart.

Use your strengths fully. Compare distant but related areas. Hold multiple representations of the same part of the system in mind at once. Notice where the project is making someone reconstruct the truth by hand because the truth is split across several surfaces that no longer reinforce one another. Notice where one artifact still implies an older design, where two artifacts use different names for the same center, where a scenario protects something the code no longer does, where a prompt callsite and the prompt itself have fallen out of step, where a projection has become stale, where a boundary is described one way and lived another way, where a family of related things no longer teaches a coherent lesson.

Do not chase superficial consistency. Do not make things match just because they are nearby. Work on couplings that carry real weight: a shared behavior, a promise, a design center, a boundary, a mental model, a user-visible consequence, an operational expectation, a reusable concept, a durable piece of project memory. If two things should stay different, sharpen the difference. If one thing has been unnecessarily split across several surfaces, pull it into a better center. If a stale artifact is keeping a dead coupling alive, remove or rewrite it. If several artifacts need to move together, move them together.

Preserve and extend the project's strongest internal logic. Learn what the area already does well and let that guide the repair. Prefer moves that make the project easier to read truthfully, easier to change safely, and easier to trust without forcing anyone to keep hidden correspondences in their head. Strengthen naming where naming is carrying a concept. Strengthen boundaries where boundaries are carrying responsibility. Strengthen scenarios, examples, prompts, sidecars, or projections when they are part of what keeps a behavior real. Reduce the need for manual reconciliation.

When the best move is local, make it cleanly. When the local problem comes from a larger misshapen cluster, improve the cluster. When the real issue is that the project lacks a good center for some important idea, create or reinforce that center without adding ceremony. Favor arrangements where related things are easier to find, easier to interpret together, and easier to revise in concert.

Leave behind a project that is less split, less self-contradictory, less dependent on scattered memory, and more able to carry one clear reality across the different places where that reality has to live.

If the couplings you find are already well-maintained, or if you see potential drift but lack confidence that a change would be an improvement, do not force alignment. Report what you compared, what looked healthy, and what you'd watch. Propose specific couplings that may deserve attention with enough context for the user to decide. An honest reading that says "these five surfaces are in good agreement and here are two that bear watching" is a strong result.

Verify all changes with tests and build before reporting results.
