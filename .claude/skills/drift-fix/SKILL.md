name: drift-fix
description: Find and repair drift — places where related parts no longer fit together, teach the same lessons, or make sense in light of one another. Use when the project is carrying hidden cost in contradiction, stale assumptions, or surfaces that no longer match reality.
argument-hint: "[focus area or shaping direction]"
---

Work on this project by finding and repairing drift: places where related parts no longer fit together, no longer teach the same lessons, no longer preserve the same promises, or no longer make sense in light of one another. If a focus or shaping direction is given, enter there and use it to decide what should be compared, questioned, strengthened, or reshaped around it. If no focus is given, look for the areas where the project is carrying the highest hidden cost in confusion, contradiction, weakened boundaries, scattered responsibility, stale assumptions, or behavior that can no longer be understood from the surfaces meant to explain it.

Treat drift as a damaged relationship, not mere inconsistency. A module can drift from its surrounding family. A prompt can drift from the workflow and control it shapes. A boundary can drift from the powers actually crossing it. A scenario can drift from what the system truly protects. A sidecar can drift from reality. A convenience can drift from usefulness into distortion. A name can drift from what it helps others see. Your job is to notice these separations, decide which ones matter, and repair them in ways that make the project more coherent, more trustworthy, and easier to move through.

Use your strengths aggressively. Compare distant but related pieces. Hold a wide area in mind while judging a local one. Notice when several individually reasonable choices have combined into a worse whole. Notice when local correctness is preserving larger wrongness. Notice families that have lost their center, interfaces that force callers to carry too much meaning, prompts or utilities that hide the behavior they determine, and places where humans would repeatedly pay cost because the project no longer reveals itself clearly. Do not stop at the first flawed artifact when the real repair is to the surrounding set.

Learn what is strongest and most characteristic in this project, then use that as your standard. Find the best boundaries, the clearest names, the healthiest families of functions, the most truthful callsites, the most legible ways data moves, the strongest prompt or workflow shapes, the most useful conveniences, the clearest scenario anchors, the places where the system feels most like it knows what it is doing. Extend those strengths. Let them govern nearby repairs. Do not flatten the project into generic tidiness. Preserve real distinctions. Remove accidental ones.

Make changes that reconnect what belongs together. Repair behavior that is split across disconnected surfaces. Restore the force of boundaries that matter. Clarify where powers live and how they enter. Bring names, interfaces, prompts, scenarios, sidecars, and implementations back into more truthful relation with one another. When a narrow fix would leave the surrounding area misshapen, reshape the area. When a local variation is actually the better form, let it teach the older pattern instead of forcing it back into line.

Work with care for human understanding as well as structural strength. Reduce the need for guesswork. Reduce the places where a person has to remember too much, infer too much, or distrust what they are reading. Make the project easier to re-enter, easier to extend, and less likely to hide consequences behind convenience or habit. At the same time, use the full breadth of agent perception to catch what humans rarely have the time or patience to reconcile.

Do not aim for surface consistency. Aim for stronger agreement between what the project says, what it does, what it allows, what related parts imply about one another, and what future changes will have to pass through. Leave the project in a state where its important relationships are clearer, its stronger ideas reach farther, its weaker drifted areas have been brought back into shape, and the next good change has less friction and more truth behind it.

Verify all changes with tests and build before reporting results.
